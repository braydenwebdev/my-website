Create experiences people remember.

Digital design today is more than making things look good—it’s about crafting interfaces and stories that feel intuitive, accessible, and meaningful. This showcase highlights work inspired by Digital Arts and web-focused learning at NWTC in Green Bay.

Whether you’re drawn to motion, typography, photography, or interaction design, you can shape your own creative direction and build a portfolio that opens doors to agencies, studios, and freelance work.

Digital arts & experience design paths

These highlights are based on real NWTC offerings in Digital Arts, Design & Graphic Technology, Web Design, and UI/UX-focused learning.

Digital Arts

Build a flexible creative career foundation. Explore digital media labs, audio and video production studios, and design tools while you experiment with illustration, motion, and storytelling.

  • Hands-on work in professional-grade labs
  • Opportunities to explore photography, video, and design
  • Pathways into freelance or in-house creative roles

Perfect if you want to try many creative tools before specializing.

Design and Graphic Technology

Combine art and technology to solve real communication problems. Learn layout, branding, and visual systems used in advertising, digital printing, and public relations.

  • Focus on typography, composition, and brand identity
  • Projects that mirror agency-style client work
  • Skills that transfer to both print and digital media

Ideal for future graphic designers and visual storytellers.

Web Design Certificate

Learn front-end web design with HTML and CSS while thinking like a UX designer. Build interfaces that look sharp on any device and support a smooth user journey.

  • Mobile-first layouts with responsive grids
  • UI patterns that support clear navigation
  • Credits that can apply toward a Web Development degree

A strong option if you enjoy both code and design.

UI/UX Design Foundations

Explore the full UI/UX process—from research and accessibility to prototyping and testing. Learn how to evaluate interfaces, design flows, and present your design decisions clearly.

  • Competitive research and user journey mapping
  • Accessibility-focused design decisions
  • Prototypes and summary reports for your portfolio

Great for anyone who loves structure, systems, and user empathy.
